Our Story

A Legacy of Engineering
Excellence

The College of Engineering, Trivandrum was established in 1939 as the first Engineering College in the then Travancore State. The first classes were started on 3rd July 1939 during the reign of the Travancore King, Sri Chithira Thirunal Balarama Varma.

Initially housed in the former office and bungalow of the Chief Engineer (present PMG Office), with Maj. T.H. Mathewman as the first Principal, the college began with an initial intake of 21 students each for Degree and Diploma courses in Civil, Mechanical, and Electrical branches.

The College was shifted to its present sprawling 125-acre campus in 1960 and has since grown to eight departments offering eight undergraduate, 27 postgraduate, and doctoral programmes with around 4,500 students.

Milestones That Define Us

From a modest beginning in 1939 to becoming one of India's premier engineering institutions.

The Beginning
Founded as the first engineering college in the Travancore State with just 21 students in Civil, Mechanical, and Electrical branches.
1960
New Campus
The college relocated to its present sprawling 125-acre campus at Kulathoor, Thiruvananthapuram — a landmark move that enabled massive expansion.
1999
Diamond Jubilee
Celebrating 60 glorious years of engineering education. The Diamond Jubilee Hall stands as a monument to this milestone.
2014
CET @75 — Platinum Jubilee
75 years of producing world-class engineers. Grand celebrations brought together alumni from across the globe.
2019
CET @80
Eight decades of academic excellence. Recognized among the top engineering colleges in India with NIRF ranking.

The Next Chapter
